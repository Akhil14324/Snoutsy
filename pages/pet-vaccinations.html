Pet Vaccinations

Protect your beloved pets from serious diseases through proper vaccination schedules and expert veterinary care.

Why Vaccinations Matter

Disease Prevention

Vaccinations protect pets from potentially fatal diseases like rabies, distemper, parvovirus, and feline leukemia.

Community Health

Vaccinated pets help prevent disease outbreaks in the community, protecting other animals and humans.

Cost-Effective

Prevention through vaccination is much more affordable than treating serious diseases that could have been prevented.

Longer, Healthier Life

Proper vaccination schedules help ensure your pet lives a longer, healthier life free from preventable diseases.

Diseases We Protect Against

Canine Distemper

Symptoms:

  • Fever, nasal discharge
  • Coughing, lethargy
  • Vomiting, diarrhea
  • Neurological signs in advanced stages

Highly contagious and often fatal viral disease affecting respiratory, gastrointestinal, and nervous systems.

Parvovirus

Symptoms:

  • Severe vomiting and diarrhea
  • Bloody stools
  • Lethargy, loss of appetite
  • Dehydration

Extremely contagious viral disease, especially dangerous for puppies. Can be fatal without prompt treatment.

Rabies

Symptoms:

  • Behavioral changes
  • Excessive drooling
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Seizures, paralysis

Fatal viral disease affecting the nervous system. Transmissible to humans. Required by law in most areas.

Feline Panleukopenia

Symptoms:

  • High fever
  • Severe diarrhea and vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Dehydration

Also known as feline distemper, this highly contagious viral disease can be fatal, especially in kittens.

Feline Upper Respiratory

Symptoms:

  • Sneezing, nasal discharge
  • Eye discharge
  • Coughing, fever
  • Loss of appetite

Common in multi-cat environments. Can become chronic and lead to serious complications.

Feline Leukemia

Symptoms:

  • Weight loss
  • Poor coat condition
  • Enlarged lymph nodes
  • Recurrent infections

Viral disease that suppresses the immune system and can lead to cancer. Spread through close contact.

Preparing for Vaccination

Schedule Appointment

Book your vaccination appointment when your pet is healthy. Avoid scheduling if your pet is sick or stressed.

Medical History

Bring your pet's medical records and inform the vet about any previous reactions or current medications.

Safe Transport

Use a secure carrier for cats and small dogs. Bring treats and comfort items to reduce stress.

Post-Vaccination Care

Monitor your pet for 24-48 hours. Provide a quiet, comfortable space and watch for any adverse reactions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are vaccines safe for my pet?

Yes, vaccines are generally very safe. The benefits of vaccination far outweigh the minimal risks. Serious adverse reactions are extremely rare (less than 0.1% of vaccinated pets).

Can my pet be vaccinated if they're sick?

No, pets should only be vaccinated when they are healthy. Vaccinating a sick pet can reduce vaccine effectiveness and stress their immune system. Wait until your pet has fully recovered.

How long does vaccine protection last?

Duration varies by vaccine type. Some vaccines provide protection for 1 year, while others can protect for 3 years or more. Your veterinarian will recommend an appropriate booster schedule based on your pet's needs and risk factors.

Do indoor pets need vaccinations?

Yes, even indoor pets need core vaccinations. They can still be exposed to diseases through open windows, other pets, or accidental escapes. Rabies vaccination is required by law regardless of indoor/outdoor status.

What if my pet misses a vaccination?

Contact your veterinarian immediately. They may recommend restarting the vaccination series or providing a booster shot. Don't delay - your pet may be vulnerable to diseases during this gap.
